Chapter 5
📖
Hello world, I'm Haresh.

I want to talk about an obvious problem that carries a non-obvious truth:

The toxic bond with authority.
👇
👨‍💼🏢
A man named Arun worked as an intern. He was hardworking, sharp, and fast.

His manager, Raj, was toxic—subtle, controlling, and dismissive.
📉🐍
One day, Arun identified a critical error that could have caused serious damage.

But during the meeting, Raj took the credit.
🌧️😔
Arun was left behind.

No recognition. No promotion.

Over time, this pushed him into depression.
😭🎉
Then came Raj's farewell party.
And here's the twist:

Arun cried.

He grieved the departure of the same person who had constantly suppressed him.
🧠🎭
Most people would call this "human nature."

But I call it something else:

The exploitation of human nature.
🏫⛓️
Why do we form attachments to people who harm us?

Because from childhood, we are conditioned to believe authority is above us. In schools, we are taught to fear teachers, not question them.
🧲⚠️
This creates a silent hierarchy where toxic behavior goes unchecked.

We don't just tolerate toxicity.

We become vulnerable to it.
⚖️🛡️
1. The Long-Term Solution:

Authority must be held accountable.
Students must be taught that authority is a role—not superiority. Questioning should be encouraged.
🛑🚶‍♂️
2. The Short-Term Solution:

Distance yourself. Detach.

Your mental stability is more important than forced loyalty.
✨🕊️
Because in the end—

Not everyone deserves your respect.

And more importantly, not everyone deserves your emotional attachment.
