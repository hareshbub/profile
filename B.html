5 Productivity Myths
That are actually killing your efficiency.

Let me break down what I've actually observed.
👇
1
🤫🎧
Myth #1: You Need Complete Silence

Senior devs? They work in the messiest environments. Open offices, Slack pings, coffee shops.

The skill isn't eliminating noise. It's training yourself to work through it.
2
🍅📊
Myth #2: You Need a Strict System

Pomodoro. Time blocking. Spreadsheets.

Here is what nobody says: if you don't actually like the system, it won't work. The real hack is finding what you'll actually stick with—even if it's weird.
3
🤹🌪️
Myth #3: Multitasking Is Bad

People who never multitask panic when things get chaotic.

People who juggle? They handle pressure better. Yes, it makes you slower initially. But that's how you build resilience.
4
🤖💻
Myth #4: AI Will Make You Productive

Blindly trusting AI outputs, copy-pasting code, using it to skip thinking?

That just moves your problem somewhere else. AI is only productive when you're still thinking critically about it.
5
🤝💸
Myth #5: Outsource Everything

Most people outsource badly. They delegate things they don't understand, get garbage back, and spend 3x longer fixing it.

Only outsource what you really understand first.
🧩
So what's the pattern?

Productivity isn't universal. It's personal.

Stop trying to fit yourself into someone else's system. Experiment. Break the rules. Find what makes you work better.
