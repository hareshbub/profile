"ADHD is a long fever—

too small to be concerned about, yet too large to be tolerated."
👇
👦🌟
Ram was an energetic, charismatic person. Widely expected to be the highest-scoring student in the state board.

During childhood, he was always punctual and consistently finished in the top ranks.
📉😟
But things changed when he reached the 10th standard.

He became lethargic, cared little about deadlines, failed his examinations, and lost his top rank.
🗣️💬
Everyone said that he had gotten distracted.

Or that he had gotten into a relationship.
🧩⚡
But the truth was completely upside down.

The reason for his downfall was not his distractions.

The reason was his ADHD.
🏆🎤
This point became perfectly clear when he won a public speaking tournament against eight different schools.

So, what is the role of ADHD here?
🎯♾️
Before 10th standard, he had a clear target and a finite amount of work.

During 10th, he faced work that never seemed to finish. And an ADHD brain prefers tasks with a finite starting and ending point.
🧬🔌
He had been burdened with allegations of distraction...

But the truth was simply his nerve wiring.

This is just one among many similar incidents in an ADHD person's life.
🛡️💪
1. Short-term Solution:

An ADHD person must be aware of their neurodivergence and seek help if needed. They must develop coping methods and lead their life with courage.
🌍📚
2. Long-term Solution:

All students across the globe must develop an awareness of personalities, psychology, and most importantly, emotional intelligence.
✨🤝
In essence...

ADHD people have a lot of potential.

But they also feel a lot of isolation in this modern society.
