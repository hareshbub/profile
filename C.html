💸
If someone asked you for one lakh rupees right now, what would you say?
👇
🛑
Obviously, no.

That's your money. You earned it. You're not just handing it over to someone who hasn't done anything for you.
⏳
But here's the thing—

Most of us do exactly that with our time.
😩
We say yes to meetings that could've been emails.

We spend hours helping people who never ask how we're doing. We commit to things we don't actually want to do, just to avoid the awkwardness of saying no.
🌌
And we treat it like it's infinite.

It's not.
🚫🕰️
Your time is literally the only resource you can't get back.

You can earn more money. You can learn new skills. But yesterday? Gone. You'll never get it back.
🤔
So why do we give it away so freely?

Because we've been taught that saying no is rude. That we should be the person who's always available, always willing to drop everything.
🧩
But that's just broken logic.

If you wouldn't give someone your money without return, why give them your time?
🧠
Next time someone asks for your time, actually think:

Will I grow from this? Will this move me closer to my goals? Or am I just doing this because I'm afraid to disappoint them?
🗣️
If the answer is "no," then say it.

Say no. Out loud.

Practice it if you have to, because most people are terrible at it.
🛡️
Saying no isn't rude. It's honest.

It's respecting yourself enough to set a boundary. And honestly? People respect you more when you do it.
✨
Start small. Say no to one thing this week.

Notice how the world doesn't end. Notice how you suddenly have time for things that actually matter.

That's when everything changes.
